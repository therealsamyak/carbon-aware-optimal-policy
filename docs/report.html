Optimal Charge Security Camera

Abstract

Carbon awareness is becoming increasingly important in the modern world, and many companies are striving to be carbon-neutral in their operations. Existing research into carbon-aware computing has focused on high-power, high-performance computing systems, but there has not been any awareness into carbon-aware battery charging, especially for small-scale IoT devices. In this paper, we propose a controller framework for a battery-powered security camera that balances the tradeoff betweenaccuracy, latency, carbon footprint, and charging decisions based on a pre-defined reward function. This controller picks the optimal models and when to charge every task interval over a horizon of time.

1. Introduction

1.1 Motivation & Objective

As climate change accelerates, organizations face mounting pressure to reduce their carbon footprint across all operations. While significant research has addressed carbon-aware computing in data centers and high-performance systems, small-scale IoT devices remain overlooked. Battery-powered security cameras represent a massive, untapped opportunity for carbon optimizationâ€”they operate continuously, consume significant energy, and often charge during peak carbon intensity periods without awareness.

Our objective is to develop an intelligent controller that optimizes both computational and charging decisions for battery-powered security cameras. The system must balance competing priorities: maintaining security performance through appropriate model selection, ensuring low latency for real-time monitoring, minimizing carbon emissions through strategic charging timing, and preserving battery longevity. This requires a holistic approach that considers the entire operational lifecycle rather than optimizing individual components in isolation.
